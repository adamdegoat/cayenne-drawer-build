Boot floor — what you can bolt to, and what NOT to drill

  • The floor is flat with a lift-out panel; a spare-wheel well sits underneath. On Bose cars the subwoofer + amp live in/around that well.
  • Do NOT drill the spare-well lid/pan, anywhere near the sub/amp, or the floor pan generally (fuel/brake lines, AWD & exhaust run beneath).
  • Anchor to the factory floor buckles / lashing eyes. Build the unit free-standing and strap it to those buckles (ratchet or cam straps) — no drilling, and it unclips and lifts out when you need the underfloor spare.
  • Keep the side compartments reachable. The boot has side bins/cubbies in the trim — that's why the unit is narrowed & centred, leaving a channel down each side. Measure your bins and set the width to clear them.
  • Keep the left-side trim panel 12V socket accessible. Put a removable deck hatch + base cutout over the well (pop the drawer above off its slides) so you can lift the floor panel and reach the spare without removing the unit — see FIG.2B. A full tyre change still needs the unit lifted out.
  • Roof load limit 100 kg; payload sits ~600–700 kg — confirm from your door-jamb plate.

Measurement checklist (do this first)

  1. Width between wheel arches at floor level and again at 300 mm up (arches lean in) — use the smaller number.
  2. Tailgate aperture width & height — the unit has to physically go in. Account for the closing seal.
  3. Seats-up floor length at the height your unit will sit (the bay tapers toward the top).
  4. Side-bin openings (both sides) — width & position; set the unit width to leave a clear channel to each.
  5. Spare-well lid + floor-panel pull-tab — mark the no-drill zone and make sure the unit lifts out / deck opens to reach it.
  6. Floor buckle positions — note where the factory lashing buckles are for your straps.
02 · The Design

Four Drawers, One Pivoting Bench

Two side-by-side columns, each split into an upper and a lower tier — four independent drawers — under one fixed flat deck. The lower tier runs on longer (over-travel) slides so it pulls out further than the upper; the left-lower is a chopping-board slide that pivots out and locks square, or lifts off to relocate. It's narrowed and centred so the boot side bins stay reachable, and strap-mounted to the floor buckles so the whole thing lifts out for the spare.

🗄️ Layout

  • Top deck (fixed): flat load / sleeping platform; lift-out section over the spare well.
  • Upper L & R: storage drawers on full-extension locking slides.
  • Lower L & R: longer over-travel slides — extend past the bumper.
  • Lower-Left = chopping board (the kitchen). Lower-Right = deep storage (stove, poles, long gear).
  • Narrowed & centred (~800 mm) so the boot side bins stay reachable down each side.
  • Strapped to the floor buckles — no drilling, and it unclips & lifts out to reach the underfloor spare.

♻ Skateboard-truck handles. One old deck gives you two trucks → two grab handles. The baseplate bolts flat to the drawer face and the hanger/axle becomes the pull bar — rugged, grippy with gloves, and a great signature detail. Spec them on the two lower drawers (the ones you yank most); use recessed flush pulls on the uppers, or find a second old board for four matching trucks. Detail sketch in the gallery below.

🛞 Spare access (option 1). A removable deck hatch + a matching cutout in the base sit over the spare-well lid. Pop the drawer above the well off its slides (and notch the mid-shelf) and you get a clear shaft straight down — lift the floor panel, inspect the spare and grab gear stored around it, all without unstrapping the unit. Cleanest when the well sits forward of the drawer boxes (measure it). Honest limit: the spare is a flat ~650 mm disc and each column is only ~370 mm wide, so the tyre itself still can't pass up through the unit — a full change means lifting the (light, strapped) unit out. Verify the birch core. Ask the yard "all-birch core or birch face on hardwood core?" and the country of origin. True void-free Baltic birch is now mostly import/special-order in Malaysia — much "birch" sold here is a birch face on a tropical-hardwood core (heavier, weaker for drawer boxes). For a drawer build, birch-faced is fine; just know what you're buying.

You'll need ≈ 2× 18mm + 1× 12mm sheets for a ~1000×850×340 unit (round up to 3× 18mm if you build drawer boxes in 18mm too). Most yards cut-to-size for ~RM1–3/cut — worth it.

05 · Hardware

Slides, Pins & Bits

The slides make or break a vehicle drawer system — buy the right ones. All prices MYR, indicative 2025/26; confirm load rating with the seller before buying the slides.

⚠ Buy the right slide. The cheap RM25 "heavy duty" 45 mm slides are soft-close cabinet runners (~35 kg, often non-locking) — not strong enough for a loaded vehicle drawer. Get the 53 mm 3-fold "2053-class" lock-in/lock-out slides (~100–120 kg). Blum doesn't make a heavy locking slide; Hafele stocks only light 30 kg locking off the shelf. True over-travel slides (Accuride 3640) are special-order via Würth/MISUMI MY (RM180–450) — most builders just use the 53 mm, which already clears the tailgate.

07 · Build Steps

Step by Step

  1. Measure & template. Cardboard-template the bay (it tapers); confirm arch width, aperture, floor length, no-drill zones.
  2. Finalise sizes. Re-draw the cut list to your real numbers, deducting 18/12mm at each joint and ~13mm per side for the slides.
  3. Cut panels (or have the yard cut). Label every piece. Dry-fit before glue.
  4. Build the carcass: sides + base + back + dead-square centre divider. Glue + screw (or pocket-holes). Square is everything — the slides reference the divider.
  5. Fit the mid shelves that split each column into upper/lower tiers.
  6. Mount slides: 53mm locking on the uppers; the longer/over-travel pair on the lowers. Mount fixed members to side + divider, level and parallel.
  7. Build the four drawer boxes (12mm); test each on its slides for smooth lock-in/lock-out.
  8. Chopping-board slide: on the lower-left, mount the HDPE board to a sub-tray via a front pivot lock-pin; add the spring detent for the 90° lock; add receiver pins for lift-off relocation.
  9. Handles: through-bolt the skateboard trucks to the two lower faces (rivnuts/bolt+washer behind); flush pulls on the uppers. Add latches.
  10. Deck + spare hatch: fit the 18mm top with a removable hatch over the spare well, a matching cutout in the base below it, and make the drawer above the well pop off its slides — so you can lift the floor panel and reach the spare without removing the unit (gas struts optional).
  11. Finish: sand, seal/varnish (or carpet the deck + faces); trim exposed edges with L-angle.
  12. Install: set on the load floor, strap to the factory floor buckles with ratchet/cam straps — do not drill the floor pan. Confirm the side bins stay reachable, the unit lifts out for the spare, the tailgate clears, and rear visibility is intact.
